Quick links to the Respiratory Therapy School at Gulf Coast State College, Panama City, FL

For those interested in

  • helping patients breathe using advanced technology
    • e.g., teach patients, give meds, run vents at up to 900 breaths per minute
    • (What is an RT?)
  • an interesting health care job after a 2 year college degree
  • a college program with
    • small classes
    • high board pass rates
    • serious education for serious students who are preparing for careers
    • non-profit state school, which focuses on your progress, not on school profits
    • inexpensive for Florida residents, competitively priced for students from other states
    • while having ... a popular salt water wind surfing park on the edge of campus
  • living in a beach resort town on the Florida Gulf Coast
    • where the water is warmer than on the Atlantic coast
    • where people can often find cheaper housing in our winter "off season"
    • where major airlines fly, including Southwest Airlines and Delta
    • where major beach resort activities are nearby
    • where quiet beaches and parks may be found
  • the potential to continue on to earn a BS degree in anything, anywhere, at any college
    • while always having an employable skill
    • earn RT wages instead of fast food wages, when moonlighting to pay for your BS degree
    • consider a BS in engineering, pre-medicine, business, whatever
    • even take a chance on a BS degree in a field where jobs are scarce (can work as an RT)
  • the potential to be employed as an RT while:
    • building a parallel career in a difficult field (e.g., become a non-starving artist)
    • working on a volunteer, charitable, or other worthwhile effort
    • raising a family
